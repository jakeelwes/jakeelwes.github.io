CUSP

2019, digital video, 10 minute loop

CUSP is a new piece exploring the intersection between nature and machine and the interplay between two realities: one generated by human perception and experience and the other generated by artificial intelligence.

A neural network trained on a dataset of birds learns the features and embeddings of different marsh birds; the differences in beak shape, feathering and feet from every instance it is shown. Unsupervised, it proceeds to plot a map without reference to human taxonomy or classification. 

Having been surrounded by birds on the Essex marshes from early childhood I was able to personally sieve through the images it was generating, picking the dreamt up birds most evocative of the marsh (specifically in) for me and then retraining it on these examples. The animation morphs/ebbs/interpolates between species/birds/images in the final network’s latent space.

The eerie images in the resulting video I then physically located on the shifting expanse of mudflats in Landermere Creek. By sinking a screen into the oozing mud at different points in the tidal channels and marshes I could observe and record how a video (consisting of artificially generated birds thrown across a screen embedded into the mud) starts to interact with the natural elements, the ways it seems to simultaneously mimic and disrupt the visual field. A strange dialogue results from systems wading across marshes.
